the maker

Yedam Park.

madey — yedam, backwards.

Yedam Park is the maker behind Madey Design. She works primarily in walnut and maple, turning each piece by hand on a lathe. Her work is shaped by a fascination with organic form — curves that feel inevitable, wood grain that tells its own story.

The pieces are functional but quietly sculptural. Cutting boards with hand-carved wavy inlays. Bubble bowls turned from alternating walnut and maple rings. Bud vases with silhouettes that look more like they grew than were made. Nothing is mass-produced. No two pieces are the same.

The name started as a small joke — madey is yedam, written backwards. It stuck because it felt honest: the work is personal, a little playful, and always made by her.

She takes a limited number of custom orders each month. If something catches your eye or you have something specific in mind, reach out directly.
