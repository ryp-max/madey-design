the maker

Yedam Park.

madey — yedam, backwards.

Yedam Park grew up in Korea with a pencil always in her hand. She drew obsessively — characters, objects, worlds — and from an early age felt the pull toward making things that could exist beyond the page. That instinct eventually led her to the University of Southern California's School of Cinematic Arts, where she studied storytelling and found a language for what she'd always been doing: using form to communicate feeling.

After USC, she worked in the animation industry and in software product design — two fields that share the same obsession with how things look, how they feel, and how they guide the people who use them. But something kept pulling her back to the physical. She wanted objects you could hold. Surfaces that changed with the light. Work that would outlast the screen it was made on.

Woodworking became the answer. Turning on a lathe is, in its own way, a kind of drawing — a continuous line, a shape emerging in real time. She works primarily in walnut and maple, drawn to the contrast between the two: dark and light, hard and soft, serious and warm. The bubble bowls, the wave-inlaid boards, the bud vases — each one starts as a sketch and ends as something you can put on your table and use every day.

Every piece is a commitment to singularity. No two will ever be the same — not because of a rule, but because the wood itself won't allow it. Grain shifts, color varies, the lathe responds differently every time. She believes in stepping back and letting the material lead: the wood does the talking, and the job is simply to listen well enough that the finished piece feels like it was always meant to be exactly where it ends up.

What drives her most isn't the making alone — it's the sharing. She loves teaching, collaborating, and the moment someone holds a piece and immediately understands it. Joy is part of the design. The work is meant to be touched, used, given away, and passed down. Pieces with a life longer than the trend cycle.

She takes a limited number of custom orders each month. If you have something in mind — a gift, a collaboration, an idea that's been sitting in your head — she'd love to hear it.
